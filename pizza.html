pizza

Description

New York pizza is renowned for its thin, hand-tossed crust, foldable slices, and unmistakable flavor. Characterized by its crispy yet chewy texture, New York-style pizza is traditionally topped with tangy tomato sauce and generous amounts of gooey mozzarella cheese. The simplicity of its ingredients allows for the true essence of the pizza to shine through, while optional toppings like pepperoni, mushrooms, or sausage provide additional layers of flavor. Whether enjoyed by the slice on-the-go or savored at a bustling pizzeria, New York pizza embodies the vibrant energy and culinary spirit of the city that never sleeps.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough: Made from flour, water, yeast, salt, and sometimes olive oil.
  • Tomato sauce: A simple yet flavorful tomato sauce made from crushed tomatoes, garlic, olive oil, salt, pepper, and sometimes herbs such as oregano and basil.
  • Mozzarella cheese: Fresh, whole milk mozzarella cheese is commonly used on New York-style pizza.
  • Toppings: While traditional New York-style pizza often features simple toppings, such as pepperoni or mushrooms
  • Olive oil: A drizzle of olive oil over the pizza before or after baking can add flavor and richness to the finished product.
  • Cornmeal or semolina flour: Used to dust the pizza peel or baking surface to prevent the dough from sticking and to give the crust a slightly crispy texture on the bottom.
